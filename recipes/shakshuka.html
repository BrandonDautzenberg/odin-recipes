Shakshuka

"My fellow Americans, I sure love me some shakshuka" ~ Barack Obama

Description

Shakshouka is a delicious combination of eggs poached in a spicy tomato sauce. Although it has an unusual name, the dish is straightforward and easy to make. Eggs are then added directly to the tomato sauce and poached until done. Merguez sausage can also be added to the dish. It is believed that shakshouka originated in Tunisia, but the dish is well-known and commonly eaten throughout North Africa and the Middle East as well. It is usually made in a skillet in which onions, tomatoes, and spices are cooked until they form a delicious tomato sauce.

Ingredients

  • one large red onion, thinly sliced
  • four eggs
  • three garlic cloves
  • two times 400 gram diced tomatoes
  • one long red chili
  • two tablespoons of sweet paprika
  • one tablespoon of sugar

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a frying pan and cook the onion for three minutes.
  2. Stir in paprika, chili and some sugar.
  3. Add the tomato and cook for 15 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Make four indents in the mixture and crack an egg in each one
  5. Reduce heat and cook for 7-8 minutes.
